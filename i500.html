What it is

An evidence base built where the data is missing.

Open Food Facts is the world’s leading open food database — 4M+ products. SCANSMART uses it as the primary backbone of CheckIT. But OFF coverage drops sharply at the independent-shop tier, especially for cultural-specific products that mainstream supermarkets don’t stock. For high-diabetes postcodes in South London where Caribbean, halal, and South Asian groceries are the daily reality, OFF can have less than 30% coverage on what’s actually in someone’s shopping basket.

The I500 fills that gap. Trained community auditors visit independent shops with shop-owner consent. Each product is scanned, photographed (with permission), label-decoded, traffic-light-rated, and added to the I500 corpus. By month 12 of operation, the I500 is the most complete dataset of independent-shop food in the UK that exists anywhere.

The name carries three readings in one letter and one number. I for Index — a structured catalogue, not a list. I for Independent — shops outside the big-grocer supply chain. I for 500 — the original cohort that anchored the catalogue’s identity, preserved in the name even as the catalogue grows past it. Each reading is independently true; together they describe what the catalogue is, who it serves, and the scale it commits to.

155 SKUs verified

Already shipping

The I500 v1 corpus carries 155 verified SKUs across South London independent shops as of 13 May 2026 — of the 148 audited against Open Food Facts, 138 (93.2%) are absent or insufficiently labelled under the strict methodology. Year-1 target: 1,000 products. Long-term ceiling: 2,000–2,500 genuinely under-represented SKUs.

Cultural specificity

Not a sub-set of OFF

The I500 is the complement to OFF, not a duplicate. Every product in the I500 is verified as not in OFF or insufficiently labelled there. Halal-certified products, Caribbean staples, South Asian household basics, kosher-specific, West African groceries.

Community-audited

Trained local auditors

Audits performed by trained community members, not contractors flown in. The auditor network is the trust layer the data inherits from. Shop owners give explicit consent via the Shop Participation Agreement.

CheckIT integrated

Live in the consumer scanner

Every I500 product is queryable via CheckIT at app.scansmart.uk. When a shopper scans a barcode that’s in the I500 but not in OFF, the I500 result is what they see. The hit rate is measured per the v4.10 Decision Record (live since 29 April 2026).

Decision Record

Behavioural signal layer

Beyond the product corpus, the I500 carries the Decision Record — the anonymous record of what shoppers tap when they see each product (Bought / Put back / Just looking). This is the put-back-pattern evidence base for behavioural intervention research.

Six Layers stack

Six dimensions of intelligence

The I500 is layer 1 of SCANSMART’s Six Layers of Intelligence Stack — product corpus, decision record, traffic-light indicator, profile-tuned thresholds, alternative-finding (SaK), audit-evidence (per Competitive Positioning Report v1.3).

Who licenses it

Five categories of buyer.

  • Retail intelligence platforms — Mintel, Kantar, Nielsen IQ. The I500 is the data they don’t have because their methodology doesn’t reach independent shops.
  • FMCG manufacturers — brands wanting to understand the cultural-specific market they currently can’t see. Halal-certified yogurt, Caribbean sauces, South Asian flours.
  • NHS commissioners + public-health teams — borough-level diet evidence for high-diabetes, high-hypertension postcodes. The I500 is the dataset for the conversation a council’s diabetes prevention team needs to have with the local independent grocers.
  • Academic researchers — nutrition, behaviour change, food-systems research. The I500 is the evidence base for studies that can’t use OFF alone because OFF doesn’t cover the population the research is about.
  • Food-tech investors + analysts — market sizing for the under-served segment. The independent-shop tier is a real commercial market that the major databases under-count by 50% or more.
How we’re funded

Independent of food manufacturers and retailers.

SCANSMART takes no money from food manufacturers, retailers, or trade bodies. That posture isn’t decorative — it’s structural. Industry-funded nutrition research favours sponsor outcomes at an odds ratio of 7.61 (Lesser et al., PLOS Medicine, 2007) and produces favourable findings 5.7× more often than independently-funded equivalents (Sacks et al., PLOS ONE, 2020). The same pattern travels with retail-intelligence data infrastructure when the funding architecture mirrors it.

The I500 was built so the figures Tier 1–3 institutional buyers (NHS / academic / retail intelligence) license carry no financial dependency on the actors the data covers. Manufacturer engagement is governed under explicit editorial-integrity constraints; commissioner-led audits are arranged through institutional enquiry. The structural-critique posture in Checkout, Decoded rests on the same foundation.
