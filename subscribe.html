Inside the institutional tier

Two sub-tiers. Different depths. Different price bands.

The institutional column above splits into two sub-tiers because institutional buyers buy data products at two different depths. The split mirrors how Bloomberg, Mintel, and Kantar structure their B2B offers; SCANSMART applies the same pattern in food-and-cosmetics-intelligence space.

Snapshot

For analysts who need to scope or scan.

Structured snapshot views of the I500 corpus by category, postcode, manufacturer cluster, condition-lens scoring distribution. Selective named-product reveals at sample level. Aggregate basket-pattern statistics. Static quarterly/annual analytical reports. Read-only access to scoped slices of the dataset.

For: public-health analysts, policy researchers, food-policy journalists, council policy officers, NGO researchers, food-focused academics, smaller commissioning bodies, FMCG analysts running pre-engagement scope.

Pricing band: mid-band institutional subscription. Mintel Reports analyst tier as the closest published comparable. Specific figure locked at v5.0 sign-off.

Full Dataset

For enterprises that need ongoing programmatic access.

Full row-level access to the I500 corpus. Ingredient-centric query layer (the inverted ingredient → products schema). Source-priority adjudicated dataset. Condition-lens scored data feeds (diabetes / hypertension / family / general). Reformulation-tracking timeseries (DHSC commitments joined to SCANSMART audit history). Custom commissioner briefs. Sector comparison reports. Developer API + SLA. Named account manager.

For: NHS Integrated Care Boards, FMCG manufacturers running reformulation tracking, retail intelligence platforms integrating I500 data, large councils, food-tech investors with diligence-shape data needs, media organisations with continuous food-investigation publishing.

Pricing band: Bloomberg-Terminal / Mintel Reports Premium / Kantar Worldpanel enterprise band. Six-figure annual at Year 3 target; tiered Year 1 launch pricing to seed institutional adoption.

Both sub-tiers serve different institutional buyers at different price bands. Both produce revenue that funds the free side — CheckIT, the Knowledge Library, the Weekly Checkout, the Shop Directory, KiP Stories. The free side does load-bearing credibility work for both paid tiers; the paid tiers monetise the granular data the free side is derived from. This is structurally identical to Bloomberg's loop applied in food-and-cosmetics-intelligence space.

Important: the per-product CheckIT scan stays free for everyone, regardless of tier. A user holding a product gets the full per-product read on that product. No paywall ever sits between a person at the moment of decision and the read on the product they hold. The institutional tier sells access to the corpus that produces the readings — not access to any individual user's reading.
