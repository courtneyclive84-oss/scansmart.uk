## The map

The shopping environment is engineered across six categories. The UK regulates a subset; the rest is market-led. In every category the upstream actor is the manufacturer who pays for the slot or designs the pack; the retailer is the cooperating surface. International precedent shows where regulation is heading.

| # | Category | Primary Mechanism | UK Regulatory Status (2026) | Upstream Actor | International Parallel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Placement** | End-caps · Checkouts · Store entrances | **Restricted (HFSS only)** — SI 2021/1368, in force October 2022 | Manufacturer pays the slotting fee; retailer arranges the floor | No equivalent statutory placement restriction in major Western markets |
| 2 | **Pricing** | BOGOF · Multibuy · Was/now anchoring · Loyalty-only pricing · Unit-price obscurement | **Partial** — HFSS multibuy restricted (SI 2021/1368, in force October 2025); other forms unregulated; unit-price disclosure required but prominence unregulated (Price Marking Order 2004) | Manufacturer underwrites the promotion budget; retailer monetises loyalty data | Maryland HB 895 (6 May 2026) bans dynamic personalised pricing in large grocery channels |
| 3 | **Visual** | Eye-level shelf positioning · Sightline design | **Unregulated** — market-led | Manufacturer pays the premium shelf inch; retailer optimises revenue per square metre | No major statutory equivalent |
| 4 | **Sensory** | Bakery aroma · Music tempo · Lighting · Layout | **Unregulated** | Retailer-led environment design; manufacturer's pack is the convertible target | No major statutory equivalent |
| 5 | **Packaging** | Front-of-pack health-halo claims · Character licensing · Mascots | **Partial** — regulated nutrition claims (EFSA register / UK retained); HFSS advertising channels restricted (ASA/CAP, in force January 2026; first enforcement April 2026); physical pack at point of sale not separately regulated | Manufacturer designs the pack as the structural carrier of the claim | Chile black-octagon warning labels (Law 20.606, 2016); Mexico (2020); WHO 2023 marketing-to-children recommendations |
| 6 | **Digital** | Homepage carousel · Basket nudges · Algorithmic recommendation slots · Personalised offers · Dynamic pricing | **Mixed** — HFSS placement extended to "equivalent online positions" under SI 2021/1368; HFSS paid online advertising restricted (ASA/CAP, January 2026); algorithmic recommendation slots and dynamic personalised pricing unregulated | Manufacturer pays the digital end-cap (carousel slot); platform algorithm enacts the slotting investment | Maryland HB 895 (May 2026); more than two dozen US states reportedly considering similar legislation |

## How to read the map

- **The Restricted rows (1, 2 partial)** are the regulatory wins of 2022–2026. They cover the highest-conversion physical-shelf triggers for HFSS products and the volume-promotion lever. They are the model the rest of the regulatory frame could grow from.
- **The Unregulated rows (3, 4)** are the triggers the law has not named. The shopper is on their own. Decoder literacy is the only mediation.
- **The Partial / Mixed rows (5, 6)** are the regulatory frontier. The advertising lane (HFSS ads, January 2026 enforcement) is hardening faster than the placement-and-personalisation lane. The digital-shelf lane is where the next generation of statutory intervention will land, with Maryland HB 895 as the named precedent.

In every row, the **upstream actor is the manufacturer**. The retailer is the cooperating surface. Naming the surface alone misses the structural play.

## Devolution note

- **England:** SI 2021/1368 in force as stated above.
- **Wales:** Equivalent placement restrictions consulted; not yet brought forward as identical-scope statutory instrument at time of writing. Watch the Welsh Food Advisory Committee meeting of 18 May 2026.
- **Scotland:** Separate consultation on HFSS promotion and marketing restrictions.
- **Northern Ireland:** Under review.

- **WHO (2010, 2023)** — Marketing of foods and non-alcoholic beverages to children; updated 2023 *Policies to Protect Children from the Harmful Impact of Food Marketing.*

## Voice rule restatement

In every row of this map, the manufacturer is the creator of the gap. The retailer is the cooperating surface. The shopper is the target. Regulation that names only the retailer surface misses the upstream play; decoder literacy that maps the trigger to the manufacturer restores it.
