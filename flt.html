The terminal · seven panels

Keyboard-first. Live data. One screen.

The Food Literacy Terminal compresses what a category manager, regulator or academic researcher needs about any barcoded product into one dense, scannable view — no dashboard tabs, no menu trees, no per-seat enterprise login. Trading-floor DNA, applied to food literacy.

<F1> PROD

Product detail

Brand, pack, image, barcode, Nutri-Score (A–E), NOVA processing (1–4), Eco-Score with carbon-footprint in g CO₂/100g. I500 records show distributor, origin, price, Wanis code, audit notes.
<F2> INGR

Ingredient breakdown

QUID percentages, E-number additives flagged by EFSA risk, full FSA-14 allergen scan with single-line ✓/⚠ summary. Visual ticks for everything absent.
<F3> NUTR

Nutrition per 100g

Macros + traffic-light dots against the UK FSA/DHSC FOP thresholds — green/amber/red on fat, saturates, sugar, salt. Year-on-year change column.
<F4> PEER

Comparable SKUs

Live category lookup across the SCANSMART universe with sparkline sugar & salt bars and one-click CSV export. Falls back to embedded universe if blocked.
<F5> CHART

Reformulation history

Time-series line chart pulled from Open Food Facts revisions — see sugar and salt move over years, with suspect data flagged red. Toggle to FOP-threshold bars with T.
<F6> RECL

Live FSA recalls

UK Food Standards Agency feed, severity-coloured: red for hazards (plastic, salmonella), amber for allergens, grey for labelling errors.
<F7> WIRE · Intelligence Rail

Live multi-stream intelligence

The seventh panel runs as a persistent rail beside F1–F6. Multi-stream live feed: filter to any combination of recalls, additive status changes, allergen alerts, reformulation flags, source health, Decision Record signal, watchlist or methodology updates. Time-sorted underneath. Click any item to load the relevant F-panel with that record. The terminal stays connected to the world.

The constellation

One terminal. Eight verified sources.

FLT is not built on any single dataset. It's the integration layer across every source that publishes UK food label intelligence — from Open Food Facts at the mainstream end to the SCANSMART I500 at the independent-grocer end, with regulator feeds, retailer-official data and manufacturer-published claims as equal citizens. The terminal reads them together.

OFF
Open Food Facts community database — ~2.8M products globally, mainstream UK retail coverage.
I500
SCANSMART verified catalogue — 155 SKUs from UK independent grocers, field-audited.
FSA
UK Food Standards Agency — live product recalls, FSA-14 allergen alerts, OFCOM-tier feed.
EFSA
European Food Safety Authority — additive safety re-evaluations, restrictions, withdrawals.
Retailer Official
Tesco, Sainsbury's, Morrisons published product feeds — where direct sources exist.
Manufacturer Official
Coca-Cola, Heinz, Unilever and others — published manufacturer data cross-referenced to label.
USDA FoodData
USDA FoodData Central — secondary cross-reference layer for nutrition macros.
OBF
Open Beauty Facts — cosmetics handoff layer (CheckIT scanner mode, per v7.2 §99).

Source priority follows the SCANSMART deterministic hierarchy (per Bible §10): I500 verified → manufacturer official → retailer official → OFF → contribution. No single source dominates the read. The terminal reconciles them all.

The completeness edge

OFF covers the mainstream. The I500 covers what OFF misses.

Open Food Facts covers the mainstream — 2.8M products globally, the workhorse for what most of the UK population buys every day. But none of OFF's community contributors go inside the independent grocers where the UK's African, Caribbean, South Asian and Eastern European diaspora actually shop. The I500 is the field-audited layer that closes that gap — 155 verified products from 19 shops, built by SCANSMART field audits, store by store, label by label. FLT brings both into one literate read.

The landscape

The label-literacy slice of what Mintel and Innova sell — on a UK regulatory layer they don't have.

Mintel, Innova and Euromonitor sell commercial intelligence: positioning claims, retail-purchase location, packaging attributes, price-at-purchase, formulation trends, two-year forecasting, across millions of SKUs in 86 countries. They're built for Fortune 500 brand managers and they're priced accordingly. FLT sells a different axis — the literacy intelligence slice: Nutri-Score, NOVA, Eco-Score, additives, FSA-14 allergens, live UK FSA recalls, reformulation history. Smaller universe. Different buyer. Public-interest layer those €40,000 tools don't ship. The moat isn't the I500 alone — it's the integration: eight verified data layers, one literate read, no competitor stitching them together.

What FLT isn't

Not volume

~1,550 SKUs today, growing. Mintel indexes millions across 86 countries. We index deeper on the SKUs we cover — verified by field audit. Different game.

Not commercial intel

No positioning claims, retail location panels, packaging attributes, price history, or forecast modelling. That's what Mintel/Innova/Circana sell. FLT sells literacy and safety intelligence.

Not equivalent

If you need full CPG market intelligence, you buy Mintel. If you need to know what's on the label, who's at risk, and what's been recalled — that's FLT. The table below is honest about which axis we own.

FLT — Food Literacy Terminal

The institutional surface for UK food labels. 148 I500 audited SKUs (155 catalogue · 138 strict blindspot vs OFF) + 1,500 OFF community SKUs. Live FSA recalls, EFSA additive safety, reformulation history, FSA-14 allergen scan, 7-panel terminal with food-news wire (13 institutional sources).

Data: I500 · OpenFoodFacts · FSA · EFSA · WHO · Lancet · Met Office · DEFRA
Next · beta H2 2026

SLT — Supplements Literacy Terminal

The same terminal-grade architecture applied to UK food supplements + vitamins. Active-ingredient decoding, MHRA borderline-medicinal-claim flags, dosage adequacy against COMA RNI/RDAs, drug-interaction alerts, manufacturer-claim verification. Different regulatory layer to food — MHRA jurisdiction, not FSA.

Targets: MHRA · NHS Yellow Card · COMA · EU NFR · Examine.com · PubMed
Next · beta Q4 2026 · B2B-only

CLT — Cosmetics Literacy Terminal

Same terminal aesthetic on UK + EU cosmetics, for professional users only. INCI-list decoding, EU CPR allergen flags, MHRA + OPSS safety alerts, hair-relaxer / skin-lightening / sunscreen vertical deep-dives already drafted in the SCANSMART research vault. The architecture already supports it — Open Beauty Facts is OFF's sister API. Consumer cosmetics scanning ships as a CheckIT mode (per v7.2 §99 patch), not as a separate consumer product.

Targets: OPSS · MHRA · EU CPR · Open Beauty Facts · CosIng · INCI

Three terminals, one architecture. Each vertical reuses the FLT engine — proprietary verified dataset + community data + live regulator feed + peer-reviewed signal + a literacy-tier presentation layer. Each gets a separate audit programme. Partner Programme conversations welcome on all three: admin@scansmart.uk.
