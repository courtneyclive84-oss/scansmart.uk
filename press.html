Story angles

Where SCANSMART sits in the news cycle.

Food, health, and the UPF debate

The UK food-policy conversation is converging on ultra-processed foods (UPFs) at exactly the moment SCANSMART is shipping a tool that decodes them. Recent peer-reviewed work — Milbank 2026 (tobacco-tactics framing), Neurology 2024 (cognitive decline cohort), UK Biobank 2025 (brain-imaging study) — gives the topic policy weight. SCANSMART’s Knowledge Library at /library is the curated entry point to that evidence.

Health inequalities and postcode-targeted intervention

Open Food Facts and major retail intelligence platforms systematically under-cover the independent shops where halal, Caribbean, South Asian, kosher, West African, and other cultural-specific groceries are stocked. In high-diabetes postcodes, this means the data-base for any household-diet research is structurally biased toward the products people in those postcodes don’t buy. The I500 fixes that gap.

NHS pathway and the HealthStore route

SCANSMART is on the NHS HealthStore pathway — DTAC V2 in progress, NICE evaluation targeted Q1 2027, listing targeted Q2–Q3 2027. The NHS App is the primary distribution channel for clinically-validated digital health tools per the NHS 10-Year Health Plan; SCANSMART is built to land there without overclaiming medical-device status.

The infrastructure-not-app investor framing

SCANSMART’s commercial model is “community-sourced, institution-monetised” — the public substrate (CheckIT, Weekly Checkout, Knowledge Library) is free and grows community trust; the institutional revenue (I500 enterprise licences, Subscriptions, Partner programmes) funds the public substrate without permanent grant dependency. This is structurally different from consumer-app companies needing growth-at-all-costs and from B2B data companies needing paywall-by-design.

Aisle-edge intervention via NFC

The Aisle Card NFC strategy (v5.2 in the platform roadmap) places NFC tags in partner shops so a shopper taps their phone to a card and the scanner opens with the barcode pre-loaded — no app installation needed. This is consumer behavioural intervention at the precise moment of decision, in the place the decision happens.
