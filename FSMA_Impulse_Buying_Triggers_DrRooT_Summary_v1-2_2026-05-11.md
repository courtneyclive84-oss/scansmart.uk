## Root-level finding

The supermarket-shelf and online-shelf trigger architecture is a manufacturer-funded retailer-executed design system. Slotting allowances, paid end-cap placement, eye-level positioning, multibuy structuring, character licensing, and the digital-shelf equivalents all share the same upstream-actor structure: the manufacturer pays for the slot, the retailer arranges the slot, the shopper occupies the position the slot was designed to capture.

This is a structural manufacturer play, not a retailer play.

---

## Cross-surface scope

The trigger architecture is not bounded by the physical store. The same five trigger categories (placement, pricing, sensory, packaging, promotional/digital) replicate across:

- **Physical-shelf surface** — end-cap, eye-level, checkout, store-entrance produce
- **Digital-shelf surface** — homepage carousel, category-page top slot, basket nudges, personalised recommendation slots
- **App surface** — push notifications, loyalty-card-only pricing, personalised offers
- **Email surface** — "you might also like" prompts, anchored pricing

The cross-surface property qualifies this brief as Dr RooT scope per §92 — root = superuser; the same naming holds across surfaces.

## Three regulatory points

1. **UK has placement regulation; UK does not have full trigger regulation.** The Food (Promotion and Placement) (England) Regulations 2021 cover entrance, end-cap, and checkout placement of HFSS products and (from October 2025) HFSS volume promotions. They do not cover eye-level positioning, sensory triggers, character licensing on the pack at point of purchase, loyalty pricing, or dynamic personalised online pricing.

2. **The HFSS advertising rules (ASA / CAP, in force January 2026) are now generating enforcement.** First rulings appeared April 2026 (banked in SCANSMART External Scan 4 May 2026). The advertising lane is hardening faster than the digital-shelf placement lane.

3. **The digital-shelf lane is the regulatory frontier.** Maryland HB 895 (6 May 2026, banked 9 May Weekly Scan item 9) prohibits dynamic personalised pricing in US grocery channels meeting size thresholds. More than two dozen US states are reportedly considering similar legislation. The UK has no equivalent. The NIQ-FMI projection of US online grocery reaching $452B by 2028 indicates the surface where regulation is least developed is also the surface where market share is growing fastest.

## Manufacturer-as-creator-of-the-gap restatement

The corrected voice canon (1 May 2026) holds across the trigger architecture without modification. The shelf is the retailer's surface; the slot is the manufacturer's purchase. The end-cap is the retailer's real estate; the product on the end-cap is the manufacturer's pack. The basket nudge is the platform's prompt; the product behind the prompt is the manufacturer's listing. In each case, the upstream design decision sits with the manufacturer; the retailer cooperates because the slot is a revenue line.

Naming the retailer alone misses the structural play. Naming the platform alone misses the structural play. Naming the manufacturer as the upstream actor and the retailer / platform as the cooperating surface is the structurally accurate move.
